Let us take a step back from what we have done so far. When you wrote the first two parts of your story, how much attention did you pay to description and setting?

Good description always includes the following:

  1. The use of all five senses.

  2. A few original figures of speech, such as metaphors and similes.

  3. A selection of details to establish the tone or mood weaved into the scene when it’s necessary.

  4. Colourful language created by using specific nouns and active verbs.

  5. The omission of superfluous adjectives and adverbs.

  6. Varied sentence structures: Shorter sentences and fragments create pace. Longer sentences slow us down.

Less is more, is better.
