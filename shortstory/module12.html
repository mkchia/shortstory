My short stories are like soft shadows I have set out in the world, faint footprints I have left. I remember exactly where I set down each and every one of them, and how I felt when I did. Short stories are like guideposts to my heart… ~Haruki Murakami

The core of your story is where most of the central conflict takes place between the protagonist and the antagonist.

However, try to show us a different, surprising aspect of your main character too. Which aspect could we never have guessed? Consider revealing it here.

Remember this could mean anything unexpected, from shock, horror or disgust, to unexpected sympathy or emotion.

Example: If you’ve set the story in a work environment, show us what his or her home looks like. Who shares it with the character? How does this detail surprise us?

Perhaps it calls for a dramatic shift in tone:

  1. If your start was humorous, how about introducing a surprising dark element?

  2. If your start was serious, how could you write this part humorously?
