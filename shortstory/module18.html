I’m a failed poet. Maybe every novelist wants to write poetry first, finds he can’t and then tries the short story which is the most demanding form after poetry. And failing at that, only then does he take up novel writing. ~William Faulkner

We have reached the end of this course. Now it is up to you to write, write and rewrite your stories. Writing is an art we spend our whole lives trying to master. Some days will be great, the writing will flow. Other days will be a disastrous with one blank page after another, but if you show up every day those days will come around less often.

  1. Write every day

    This is still the best advice. Athletes, artists and musicians practice and practice and then they start again. Writers are no different.

  2. Experiment

    Keep it exciting. Try new prompts, change viewpoint, tense and gender roles.

    Keep monotony at bay by using as much variety as possible.

  3. Enter competitions

    Competitions have deadlines. We’re writers we need those.

  4. Set goals and deadlines

    Decide how many stories you want to write in a week, a month or year. Make a plan and and stick to it.
